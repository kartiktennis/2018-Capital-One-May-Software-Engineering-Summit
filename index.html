As seen to the left, Hayes Valley, Embarcadero South, and Presidio are the areas with the longest dispatch times. Because of this, victims of incidents in these areas are forced to wait longer for help to arrive. To combat this problem, there are a numerous measures that can be implemented by the SFPD. For example, lets look at Presidio and Embarcadero South. As displayed on the map, these two locations are the farthest away from the Mission District Police Station (MDPS) (often referred to as the heart of the city), each 18-21 mins away. This is undoubtedly the most likely reason for the longer dispatch times. To fix this, the SFPD should establish small scale unit stations that are closer to these areas to lessen the distance and the time needed to respond to these incidents. For Hayes Valley, even though only 7 mins away from MDPS, the crowded streets lengthen the dispatch time. To lessen this, the SFPD should consider sectioning off some kind of main detour route that is inaccessible by civilians and solely reserved for guiding responders.
